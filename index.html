Exploring language, interaction, and culture

I'm a Ph.D. candidate at UCLA in the Department of Asian Languages and Cultures.

My main interest is collecting and analyzing conversations with a focus on when and how speakers accomplish different social actions (speech acts) with multimodal resources in conversations, such as grammar, prosody, gestures, etc.

I'm also interested in foreign language pedagogy (ESL, Chinese as a Second Language). I've been teaching all levels of Mandarin Chinese since 2012. 

In my non-academic life, I write about strategies to improve communication skills, cook, hike, and take photos of random people and objects.

This site includes my reserach & teaching background (CV), as well as resources you might (or might not) find helpful.

Research projects

Epistemic stance in Mandarin

You might have noticed this, people say "maybe" a lot! My research shows that the Chinese counterpart of "maybe", 可能, is used even more frequently than English, which could means Chinese are less committed to their positions in everyday conversation!
Check out my paper: 
What are speakers doing when they pretend to be uncertain?: Actions with non-committal epistemic stance in Mandarin Conversation?

Promise-making in institutional and everyday conversation

How "big" should your promises be? It seems like the more severe the consequences are, the "bigger" promises people make! My paper: 
The principle of proportionality: Consequentiality and promises in Mandarin conversation. Chinese language and discourse,11 (forthcoming).

This is also my dissertation topic! Looking forward to more findings!

Inclusivity in Chinese as a second language classroom

We are editing a special issue for the journal of Chinese as a Second Language (CSL), including my paper on exposure to non-standard Chinese accent.

Interaction between Chinese politicians and journalists

A long term inter-disciplinary project that aims to reveal patterns of political discourse and media communication in People's Republic of China.
Paper in progress:  Negotiation of stance and identity through second person pronouns: journalistic role in China

Teaching

1

Asian Language Pedagogy

This course investigates major issues in teaching and learning of Asian languages. Students will learn pedagogical grammar, curricular development, social, cultural, and cognitive foundations of Asian language acquisition, best practices in teaching Asian language writing systems, special issues in teaching heritage students, comparisons of K-12 language teaching and college language teaching, assessment methods, and emerging trends in Asian language teaching. Students will acquire practical teaching skills through classroom observation and teaching demo.

Instructor: Prof. Sung-Ock S. Sohn

2

Topics in Chinese Language and Culture

This course explores the inter-dependent relationship between the Chinese language and culture.  Students will learn to use basic concepts in socio-cultural linguistics, Discourse Analysis, as well as technology to analyze the Chinese language and the cultural conventions expressed through verbal and non-verbal linguistic devices. Major coverage will be on language use as reflected in everyday interaction as well as in various types of media: film, television, internet, advertisement, and so forth.

Instructor: Prof. Hongyin Tao

3

Introduction to Chinese linguistics

This is an introductory course of Chinese linguistics. Students will learn the sound system, writing system, and its reform, regional differences, major structural features of Chinese. Students will also explore Chinese languages in society and in cultural practices. 
​
Instructor: Wei Wang/Helen Wan
